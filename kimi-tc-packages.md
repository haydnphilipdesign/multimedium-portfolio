## Answers to Your 10 Questions

### 1. Are my current three packages structured well, or would a four-tier structure make more sense?

**Four tiers makes more sense** for this market. The TC audience has a wider spread of readiness and budget than your current 3 tiers can capture without forcing people into an oversized first purchase.

The current gap — $750 to $1,500 to $3,000+ — assumes the buyer is either "new but has $750" or "established with $1,500." In reality, many new TCs have maybe $400-600 in budget and zero deal flow yet. They are not cheap; they are just early. If you do not serve them, they go to Wix or a random Fiverr designer. That is fine if you truly do not want them, but since you are building a referral network and want to own the TC niche, it is strategically smart to have a **productized on-ramp** that catches them, delivers value fast, and primes them for an upgrade.

### 2. Is $750 too high as the lowest entry point for this particular TC audience, or is it okay if I'm intentionally positioning above low-budget buyers?

**Yes, $750 is too high as the absolute floor** if you want to capture decent beginner TCs who were referred to you. It is not too high if you only want TCs who already have active income. But here is the nuance: the woman who balked probably did not balk because she is cheap. She balked because she mentally compared your "$750 simple one-page site" to a "$15/month Wix site" and could not bridge the gap. You are not losing her because she has no money; you are losing her because you have not closed the value-perception gap at the entry level.

So the issue is not "should I serve $250 buyers?" The issue is **your floor package does not have a clear enough value ladder beneath it to make $750 feel like the obvious middle choice.**

### 3. Would adding a $495 or $595 "credibility site" help me capture more good-fit beginners, or would it risk cheapening the offer?

**A $595 tier will help, not hurt, IF you build hard walls around it.** The danger is not the price point. The danger is a low-priced tier that still requires custom design decisions, revision rounds, and hand-holding. That would cheapen your brand and eat your margin.

Do this instead: make the $595 tier explicitly a **"template-based presence"** — not a custom design project. Pre-built layout. Pick from 3 hero styles. Your colors and logo applied. Guided copy. One week. No revision round, just a refinement pass. That positions it as "get online fast" rather than "cheap custom website." It protects your positioning because you are not discounting custom work; you are selling a different, faster product.

**Yes, restructure so $750 is explicitly "site + intake."** Your current Starter at $750 is actually underpriced for what it includes (custom design, copy, link preview, hosting). Adding intake form embedding at $750 makes it suddenly feel like a business tool, not just a brochure.

**Lower Pro to $2,495.** "$3,000+" is a heavy psychological wall for a solo operator selling to small service businesses. The jump from $1,500 to $3,000+ is a $1,500+ upsell, which often triggers "I need to think about this" or committee decision-making.

At $2,495 you still anchor high. You do not need the "+" to look premium; your copy and demos do that work.

### 8. How should I explain the value of these packages better so that a new TC understands why this is not just "a simple one-page website"?
